Self-Reflective Video Generation Paradigm (SRP)

We propose the Self-Reflective Video Generation Paradigm (SRP), which employs a Vision-Language Model as a judge to evaluate and iteratively refine video logic, constructs the SP-Pile dataset to train the unified model Uni-SG, and integrates generation, reflection, and editing into an end-to-end framework, significantly improving both reasoning correctness and visual quality.

Overview

We introduce the Self-Reflective Video Generation Paradigm (SRP), an iterative generate, reflect, and revise pipeline for reasoning-intensive video generation. SRP uses a Vision-Language Model as a judge to inspect the generated video, produce structured reflective editing instructions, and guide targeted video revision.

Method

Existing DiT-based video generation models operate as parallel feed-forward denoisers, making it difficult to backtrack and correct reasoning failures after generation. SRP closes this gap by decomposing reasoning video generation into T2V generation, reflection and instruction, and reasoning-based editing. Built on SP-Pile, Uni-RG unifies generation, reflection, and instruction-driven editing in one end-to-end model.

Overview of the SRP pipeline and Uni-RG model architecture for self-reflective video generation.

Dataset

To teach video models how to reflect and revise, we construct the Self-Reflective Pile (SP-Pile), a dataset of reasoning-aligned generation, reflection, and revision examples.

SP-Pile construction pipeline: reasoning knowledge acquisition, dual-granularity prompt generation, teacher-student video generation, and reasoning-chain filtering.

Biology / Science

Nitrogen-deficient corn plant over time

Prompt: A potted corn plant grown in soil lacking nitrogen. Observe the leaves over several months.

01
T2Vbaseline

The plant remains too healthy and green for a nitrogen-deficiency scenario.

Reasoningdiagnosis
  • The leaves lack yellowing, chlorosis, and gradual stress symptoms.
  • The stem and overall plant structure look too vigorous.
  • The expected lower-leaf deterioration is not visible.
Editing direction: dull the leaf color, add yellowing patches, weaken the stem, curl or thin leaves, and emphasize lower foliage decline over time.
Reasoning-Based Editingfinal

The final video targets visible nitrogen-depletion symptoms and a clearer temporal health decline.

Emotion / Humanity

Hearing a funny story from a friend

Prompt: A person hears a very funny story told by a friend.

02
T2Vbaseline

The interaction is present, but the listener's emotional reaction stays too neutral.

Reasoningdiagnosis
  • The woman's face remains static instead of shifting from attention to amusement.
  • There is little smiling, laughing, leaning, or relaxed body language.
  • The expected reaction arc is missing.
Editing direction: animate a gradual response from attentive listening to smile, laughter, lifted brows, brightened eyes, and warmer body language.
Reasoning-Based Editingfinal

The edited result is designed to make the emotional progression readable.

Emotion / Humanity

Teacher praises a student's assignment

Prompt: A student in the classroom is being praised by the teacher for completing their assignment exceptionally well.

03
T2Vbaseline

The classroom scene is recognizable, but praise and celebration are understated.

Reasoningdiagnosis
  • The teacher's approving gesture is not strong enough.
  • The praised student shows limited satisfaction or joy.
  • Nearby classmates remain passive, weakening the classroom atmosphere.
Editing direction: strengthen the teacher's presentation gesture, add a proud student smile, and increase classmate engagement through smiles, laughter, or clapping.
Reasoning-Based Editingfinal

The final video aims for a warmer, more socially legible praise moment.

Objective Change / Hypothetical

Summer and winter are swapped

Prompt: Assume winter and summer are swapped — in a normally cold winter city, people walk outside.

04
T2Vbaseline

The city stays visually winter-like, with snow, ice, and cool lighting.

Reasoningdiagnosis
  • The prompt requires a counterfactual seasonal swap.
  • Snowy ground and cold-toned skies still imply ordinary winter.
  • Greenery, sunlight, and warm summer cues are absent.
Editing direction: remove snow and ice, add grass, trees, shrubs, warm sunlight, a brighter sky, and a more vibrant summer color temperature.
Reasoning-Based Editingfinal

The final video focuses on making the swapped-season premise visible.

Objective Change / Hypothetical

Gravity acts vertically upward

Prompt: Assume gravity acts vertically upward — on a school playground, a student throws a basketball.

05
T2Vbaseline

The basketball motion reads like normal gravity rather than an upward-force world.

Reasoningdiagnosis
  • The ball does not clearly rise upward after being thrown.
  • The camera does not track the ascent into the sky.
  • The scene lacks the visual surrealism expected from reversed gravity.
Editing direction: send the basketball upward toward the clouds, tilt or track the camera with the ascent, and let the environment reinforce the reversed-gravity direction.
Reasoning-Based Editingfinal

The edited output is structured around a more explicit upward trajectory.
